Gene Ochsenreiter Lifetime Achievement Award

Since 2000, the Mountain Amateur Athletic Club has recognized and honored a Western North Carolina community leader involved in athletics with the Gene Ochsenreiter Lifetime Achievement Award.

The honor, originally named the MAAC Lifetime Achievement Award, was renamed in 2008 to honor Ochsenreiter, a former Asheville mayor who was instrumental in creating and sustaining both the MAAC and the WNC Sports Awards Banquet, which has recognized the top area athletes and awarded scholarships since 1957. The award, sponsored by Moog Music, is presented each year at the banquet.

  • 2019: Gene Hammonds, Asheville High coach and Buddy Baldwin, Roberson coach
  • 2018: Gene McClure, Erwin statistician
  • 2017: Phil Lytle, semi-pro football coach and mentor and Robbinsville High football program (Dynasty Award winner)
  • 2016: Mike Gore, sports information director, UNC Asheville and Steve White, sports information director, WCU
  • 2015: Jamie Blue and Eddie Dalton, community volunteers
  • 2014: Keith Jarrett, Asheville Citizen-Times journalist
  • 2010: Jim Drummond, community volunteer
  • 2009: Bob Adams, golf volunteer
  • 2008: Tom Ruffing, tennis volunteer
  • 2007: Zeb Lee, Radio broadcaster
  • 2006: Bob Terrell, Asheville Citizen-Times journalist and Owen High girls basketball program, 1964-70 (Dynasty Award winner)
  • 2005: Betsy Montgomery, swimming coach and Dr. Wayne Montgomery, community volunteer
  • 2004: Joel W. Wright Jr., football player, community leader and Wilbur Eggleston, football coach
  • 2003: Bill Churm, football coach, volunteer and Albert (Tuck) Gudger, athlete and community leader
  • 2002: Dave Hart, football coach, athletic director, community volunteer; Dr. David Cappiello, football player/team physician; and J.D. Jackson, community leader, volunteer
  • 2001: Fleming Talman and H.H. “Zeugie” Zeugner, civic and community leaders
  • 2000: Norris Jones, football coach
